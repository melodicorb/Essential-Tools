How to Use the Flashcards Tool

This flashcards tool helps you create and study digital flashcards to improve your memory and learning efficiency. Here's how to use it:

Getting Started

×
×
×
  • Click "Create New Deck" to start a new collection of flashcards.
  • Give your deck a name related to the subject you're studying.
  • Use the dropdown menu to switch between different decks you've created.

Creating and Managing Cards

  • Click "Add Card" to create a new flashcard in your selected deck.
  • Enter text for both the front (question) and back (answer) of the card.
  • Edit or delete existing cards by using the buttons on each card.
  • Your cards are automatically saved to your browser's local storage.

Study Mode

  • Click "Study" to enter study mode with your current deck.
  • Click on a card or use the "Flip Card" button to reveal the answer.
  • Navigate through your cards using the "Previous" and "Next" buttons.
  • Exit study mode at any time to return to the card management view.

Frequently Asked Questions

Flashcards are learning tools that contain a question on one side and the answer on the other. They're effective because they use active recall testing and spaced repetition, which are proven techniques for moving information from short-term to long-term memory.

Yes, all your flashcards and decks are automatically saved to your browser's local storage. They will remain available when you return to this page, even after closing your browser. However, they are specific to the device and browser you're using.

Currently, the tool doesn't support exporting or sharing flashcards directly. However, you can take screenshots of your cards or manually copy the content to share with others. We're working on adding export and import features in future updates.

There's no hard limit on the number of flashcards you can create. However, since they're stored in your browser's local storage, which has limited space (usually 5-10MB), we recommend keeping your total number of cards under a few thousand for optimal performance.

For effective flashcards, keep information concise, use your own words, focus on one concept per card, and include visual cues when possible. Study regularly with spaced repetition for best results.
