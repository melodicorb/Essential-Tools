Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good typing speed?

The average typing speed is around 40 WPM (Words Per Minute), with professional typists reaching 65-75 WPM. Anything above 60 WPM is considered good, while speeds over 100 WPM are exceptional. However, what matters most is consistent improvement and maintaining accuracy.

How is WPM calculated?

WPM (Words Per Minute) is calculated by dividing the number of characters typed (including spaces) by 5, and then dividing by the time taken in minutes. The standard assumption is that an average word is 5 characters long, which is why we divide by 5.

How can I improve my typing speed?

To improve your typing speed: practice regularly, use proper finger positioning (touch typing), focus on accuracy before speed, take timed tests, learn keyboard shortcuts, and gradually increase difficulty. Consistent practice is more effective than occasional long sessions.

Does the type of keyboard matter?

While the type of keyboard can affect comfort and potentially speed, typing skills are more important than equipment. Mechanical keyboards are popular among fast typists for their tactile feedback, but you can achieve high speeds on any keyboard with practice. Find what feels comfortable for you.
